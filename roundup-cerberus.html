Helllloooooo, Colorado and beyond. It's Friday, the twilight between the week and the weekend, where the best laid plans for how to wring every drop of joy out of your 48 hours of freedom solidify from thought to action. So here's the question: What are you up to?

You could hit one of Colorado's national parks (admission is free and we have a great little guide on how to take advantage) or add some fragrance to your Colorado garden or even check out some lions, tigers and bears for free at the Wild Animal Sanctuary just of I-76 (among other deals for the week ahead).

Or — and this is a perfectly acceptable answer — you could just hang out at the house and bingewatch all the ’80s seasons of Unsolved Mysteries.

OK, we have too much news to go down a Robert Stack rabbit hole right now. Let's get to it already.

Colorado could see double-digit health insurance hikes if action isn't taken soon

“Uncertainty around the individual insurance market for 2018 is making everyone nervous. And ultimately, this uncertainty is going to hurt Colorado consumers.”

Marguerite Salazar, Colorado Insurance Commissioner

The Affordable Care Act, no matter where you land on the political aisle, needs help. While Republicans are having lukewarm conversations about another repeal/replace effort, Colorado's insurers are warning that continued inaction — either a new plan or fixes to stabilize the current system — could directly cause rates to skyrocket in 2018. John Ingold goes deep into the circumstances causing the uncertainty and the actions that could be taken to stabilize the market.
